Which of the following options is correct for the explaining the given fact?

We have a corpus of some facts available in form of triplets, e.g. (New York, is contained in, USA). Here ‘New York’ and ‘USA’ are entities related by the relation ‘is contained in’. However, this corpus is not complete and often it is possible to predict new facts from the existing corpus.

In this task, you will be presented with one such prediction followed by two plausible explanations for the prediction generated by two AI systems (in random order). These explanations may not always imply the prediction, but they may increase our confidence about it.
Your job is to select which of the two explanations is better.
In case only one explanation is generated, you need to choose whether it is more helpful than having no explanation or not.

Please note the following -

  1. Hyperlinks on the entities would take you to the corresponding wiki page if it exists, otherwise, you will land on Wikipedia home page.
  2. Whenever an explanation says that an entity is "similar" to another entity, then hover over it to see why the AI system believes so. It lists the facts which makes it think why they are similar. These facts are present in the original corpus.
  3. Whenever an explanation says "frequently seen with", then hover over it to see other facts from the corpus in which they appear together. Again, as mentioned before, the facts are from the corpus. For brevity, we state few facts about why the AI system thinks they are similar and you get the expanded list if you hover over "and x more..."
  4. Sometimes the similarity (frequently) reason might be an empty list, in which case model is unable to say why it thinks they are similar (frequent). Of course, the model may also be wrong.
